History

River Rapids Adventures: A Journey Through Time Founding and Early Years (1970s-1980s) River Rapids Adventures was founded in 1975 by John and Mary Thompson, avid outdoor enthusiasts with a passion for whitewater rafting. Inspired by the growing popularity of the sport, they started the company with a single raft and a dream to share the thrill of the rapids with others. Their first trips were on the Snake River in Wyoming, where they quickly gained a reputation for safety and excitement. Expansion and Innovation (1990s) By the 1990s, River Rapids Adventures had expanded its operations to include multiple rivers across the United States. The company invested in state-of-the-art equipment and rigorous training programs for their guides, ensuring top-notch safety and customer satisfaction. They also introduced themed rafting trips, such as family-friendly adventures and extreme whitewater challenges, catering to a diverse clientele. Environmental Stewardship (2000s) In the early 2000s, River Rapids Adventures became a leader in eco-friendly tourism. They implemented sustainable practices, such as using biodegradable products and supporting river conservation efforts. The company partnered with environmental organizations to promote the protection of natural waterways and educate the public about the importance of preserving these ecosystems. Modern Era and Global Reach (2010s-Present) Today, River Rapids Adventures is a globally recognized brand, offering rafting trips on some of the world’s most famous rivers, including the Zambezi in Africa and the Futaleufú in South America. The company continues to innovate with new adventure packages, including multi-day expeditions and luxury rafting experiences. Their commitment to safety, sustainability, and unforgettable adventures remains at the core of their operations.
